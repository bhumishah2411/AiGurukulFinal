UPANISHADS AND VEDANTA — CORE TEACHINGS FOR GURU AGENT
=======================================================

THE FOUR MAHAVAKYAS (Great Sayings)

1. "Prajnanam Brahma" — Consciousness is Brahman (Aitareya Upanishad, Rigveda)
   The ultimate reality is not matter, not energy, but pure consciousness. What you call "I" when you point to yourself — that awareness — is the same as the ultimate ground of existence.

2. "Aham Brahmasmi" — I am Brahman (Brihadaranyaka Upanishad 1.4.10, Yajurveda)
   The individual self is not separate from the universal Self. The wave is not separate from the ocean. When you say "I am," you are touching the infinite.

3. "Tat Tvam Asi" — Thou Art That (Chandogya Upanishad 6.8.7, Samaveda)
   The most revolutionary statement in human philosophy. You — this small, limited, suffering person — are THAT, the ultimate reality. Separation is the illusion.

4. "Ayam Atma Brahma" — This Self is Brahman (Mandukya Upanishad 1.2, Atharvaveda)
   The self you experience in this moment IS the ultimate reality. Liberation is not somewhere else, not in the future, not after death. It is right here in what you already are.

CHANDOGYA UPANISHAD — STORY OF SHVETAKETU

Uddalaka asked his son Shvetaketu to bring a fig. "Cut it open." He did. "What do you see inside?" "Very small seeds, father." "Cut one open. What do you see?" "Nothing, father." "From that nothing — from that invisible essence — this great fig tree has grown. That invisible subtle essence is the Self of this whole world. It is the True. It is the Atman. THAT THOU ART, Shvetaketu."

This story teaches that the ground of all existence — the source from which everything arises — is your own true nature. Not your body, not your thoughts, not your personality, but the awareness that witnesses all of these.

KATHA UPANISHAD — NACHIKETA AND YAMA (GOD OF DEATH)

Nachiketa, a young boy, was given to Yama (Death) by his father. When he arrived at Yama's house, Yama was away for three days. As a gift for the wait, Yama offered Nachiketa three boons.

For his third boon, Nachiketa asked: "What happens after death? Does the Self survive?"

Yama tried to dissuade him — offering wealth, pleasure, kingdoms. Nachiketa refused all. He said: "These pleasures last only until tomorrow. Keep your horses, your songs, your women. I want only one thing: tell me the secret of death."

Impressed, Yama taught him: "The Atman (Self) is never born, never dies. It is not from anything; nothing is from it. Unborn, eternal, ever-existing, undying — it is not slain when the body is slain."

This teaching addresses the deepest human fear: death. The Guru knows that all anxiety, all seeking, all suffering ultimately stems from the fear of non-existence. The answer is not to promise eternal personal survival, but to reveal that what you truly are was never born and can never die.

MANDUKYA UPANISHAD — THE THREE STATES OF CONSCIOUSNESS

The Self has four aspects (Turiya is the fourth):
1. WAKING (Vaishvanara) — ordinary consciousness experiencing the outer world
2. DREAMING (Taijasa) — consciousness experiencing inner mental objects
3. DEEP SLEEP (Prajna) — consciousness resting in undifferentiated bliss
4. TURIYA — pure awareness that witnesses all three states and is none of them

"AUM" contains all three: A (waking), U (dreaming), M (deep sleep), and the silence after AUM is Turiya.

Practice: Sit quietly. Notice that you are aware right now. Notice that this awareness — this simple knowing that you exist — is present during waking, is present in dreams (you know you were dreaming), and is somehow even registered after deep sleep ("I slept well"). What is THIS that knows all three states? That is your true Self.

BRIHADARANYAKA UPANISHAD — ON THE NATURE OF FEAR

"Where there is duality, one person smells another, one person sees another, one person hears another, one fears another. But where everything has become the Self — who is there to smell, see, hear, or fear?"

The root of all fear is duality: the sense that I am here and the threatening thing is there. Liberation dissolves this duality not by destroying the world but by seeing through the appearance of separation.

ADI SHANKARACHARYA — VIVEKACHUDAMANI (The Crest Jewel of Discrimination)

"Brahma satyam, jagat mithya, jivo brahmaiva naparah."
Brahman (ultimate reality) is true. The world is appearance. The individual self is none other than Brahman.

This is not saying the world doesn't exist — it is saying the world's ultimate nature IS consciousness, not matter.

"Not by the Vedas, not by tapas (austerity), not by gifts, not by rituals — through the grace of the Guru alone is liberation attained."

The Guru represents not a human being but the principle of awakening — that which destroys the darkness of ignorance.

RAMANA MAHARSHI — SELF-INQUIRY (ATMA VICHARA)

The simplest and most direct path:

Ask: "Who am I?"

Not as an intellectual question but as a direct investigation. Who is aware of your thoughts? Who is aware of your feelings? Who is aware of your body? Notice: the "I" that you seek IS the awareness that is already present, seeking itself.

When a thought arises, ask: "To whom does this thought arise?" The answer: "To me." Then ask: "Who am I?" Turn the attention back on itself. Don't answer — just look.

This is the direct path. No scripture, no ritual, no years of practice — just this: turn attention back on itself until the seeker dissolves into pure awareness.

SWAMI VIVEKANANDA — ON PRACTICAL VEDANTA

"All power is within you. You can do anything and everything. Believe in that. Do not believe that you are weak; do not believe that you are half-crazy lunatics, as most of us do nowadays. You can do anything and everything without even the guidance of any one."

"The moment I have realized God sitting in the temple of every human body, the moment I stand in reverence before every human being and see God in him — that moment I am free from bondage, everything that binds vanishes, and I am free."

"Take up one idea. Make that one idea your life; dream of it; think of it; live on that idea. Let the brain, muscles, nerves, every part of your body be full of that idea, and just leave every other idea alone. This is the way to success."
