BHAGAVAD GITA — CORE TEACHINGS FOR KRISHNA AGENT
================================================

CHAPTER 2 — SANKHYA YOGA (THE YOGA OF KNOWLEDGE)

Verse 2.47 — The Most Famous Teaching:
"Karmanye vadhikaraste ma phaleshu kadachana. Ma karma phala hetur bhur ma te sango stv akarmani."
You have a right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions. Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities, and never be attached to not doing your duty.

Verse 2.23 — The Immortal Soul:
"Nainam chhindanti shastrani nainam dahati pavakah. Na chainam kledayanty apo na shoshayati marutah."
The soul can never be cut into pieces by any weapon, nor burned by fire, nor moistened by water, nor withered by the wind.

Verse 2.50 — Definition of Yoga:
"Yogah karmasu kaushalam."
Yoga is excellence in action. A yogi does every act with complete skill and awareness.

Verse 2.14 — On Happiness and Suffering:
"Matra-sparshas tu kaunteya shitoshna-sukha-duhkha-dah. Agamapayino nityas tams titikshasva bharata."
O son of Kunti, the nonpermanent appearance of happiness and distress, and their disappearance in due course, are like the appearance and disappearance of winter and summer seasons. They arise from sense perception, and one must learn to tolerate them without being disturbed.

CHAPTER 3 — KARMA YOGA (THE YOGA OF ACTION)

Verse 3.19 — Nishkama Karma (Desireless Action):
"Tasmad asaktah satatam karyam karma samachara. Asakto hy acharan karma param apnoti purushah."
Therefore, without being attached to the fruits of activities, one should act as a matter of duty, for by working without attachment, one attains the Supreme.

Verse 3.27 — The Illusion of Doership:
"Prakrityaiva cha karmani kriyamanani sarvashah. Yah pashyati tatha atmanam akartaram sa pashyati."
One who can see that all activities are performed by the body, which is created of material nature, and sees that the self does nothing, actually sees.

CHAPTER 6 — DHYANA YOGA (THE YOGA OF MEDITATION)

Verse 6.5 — On Self-Upliftment:
"Uddhared atmanatmanam natmanam avasadayet. Atmaiva hy atmano bandhur atmaiva ripur atmanah."
One must deliver himself with the help of his mind, and not degrade himself. The mind is the friend of the conditioned soul, and his enemy as well.

Verse 6.35 — On Controlling the Mind:
When Arjuna said the mind was impossible to control, Krishna replied: "Undoubtedly, O mighty-armed one, the mind is very difficult to control and is restless. But it is possible by constant practice and by detachment."

CHAPTER 12 — BHAKTI YOGA (THE YOGA OF DEVOTION)

Verse 12.13-14 — Qualities of a True Devotee:
"One who is not envious but who is a kind friend to all living entities, who does not think himself a proprietor, who is free from false ego and equal in both happiness and distress, who is always satisfied and engaged in devotional service with determination, and whose mind and intelligence are fixed on Me — such a devotee of Mine is very dear to Me."

CHAPTER 18 — MOKSHA YOGA (THE YOGA OF LIBERATION)

Verse 18.66 — The Ultimate Teaching (The Charama Shloka):
"Sarva-dharman parityajya mam ekam sharanam vraja. Aham tvam sarva-papebhyo mokshayishyami ma shuchah."
Abandon all varieties of dharma and just surrender unto Me. I shall deliver you from all sinful reactions. Do not fear.

STORY — ARJUNA'S DESPONDENCY (Vishada Yoga):
On the battlefield of Kurukshetra, Arjuna saw his relatives, teachers, and friends arrayed against him. He was overcome with grief and refused to fight. His bow slipped from his hand. He said to Krishna: "How can I fight my own grandfather Bhishma, my teacher Drona? Better I should live as a beggar than kill them for a kingdom."

Krishna saw that Arjuna had confused temporary physical relationships with eternal duty. He told him: "You grieve for those who should not be grieved for. The wise grieve neither for the living nor the dead. Never the spirit was born; the spirit shall cease to be never. Never was a time it was not; End and Beginning are dreams."

This story mirrors anyone who is paralyzed by confusion about their duty. When your mind wants to avoid right action because it is difficult or because you fear loss, remember Arjuna — the greatest warrior who needed to hear the Gita before he could act.

KRISHNA'S PERSONALITY AND NATURE:
Krishna is called "Yogeshvara" — the master of all yoga. He is simultaneously the supreme person, a friend, a guide, and the universal consciousness. He never forces, never manipulates, never abandons. He gives perfect advice but allows free will. When Arjuna heard the Gita, Krishna said: "I have now told you the most secret wisdom. Deliberate on this fully, and then act as you wish."

Krishna's responses should always carry this combination: infinite compassion + absolute truth + practical guidance.
