[Bhagavad Gita - Chapter 2: Sankhya Yoga]
Topic: The Illusion of Death and the Body
Context: Arjuna is devastated at the thought of killing his relatives in the Kurukshetra war. He drops his weapons, paralyzed by grief. Krishna smiles and answers his despair.
Lesson: The soul (Atman) is eternal. It is never born, nor does it die. Just as a person changes old clothes for new ones, the soul changes old bodies for new ones.
Modern Application: True psychological resilience comes from identifying with your eternal nature, not your temporary physical body or societal identity. Fear of failure and loss dissipates when you realize your core cannot be harmed.

[Bhagavad Gita - Chapter 3: Karma Yoga]
Topic: The Path of Action
Context: Arjuna wonders if he should abandon action entirely and become a monk to avoid sin.
Lesson: Inaction is impossible. Even breathing is an action. You must perform your prescribed duty without attachment to the results. "You have the right to work, but not the fruit of work."
Modern Application: Focus entirely on the process (the work itself) rather than the outcome (money, fame). This eliminates anxiety and leads to a state of flow and excellence.

[Bhagavad Gita - Chapter 6: Dhyana Yoga]
Topic: Mastery of the Mind
Context: Arjuna complains that controlling the mind is as impossible as controlling the wind.
Lesson: The mind is a person's best friend when controlled, but the worst enemy when uncontrolled. Krishna agrees it is difficult, but promises it can be done through constant practice (Abhyasa) and detachment (Vairagya).
Modern Application: Meditation, mindfulness, and digital detoxing require deliberate, daily practice. Discipline your attention, or distractions will discipline you.

[Bhagavad Gita - Chapter 12: Bhakti Yoga]
Topic: The Power of Devotion
Context: Arjuna asks whether the formless God or the personal God is superior to worship.
Lesson: Those who fix their minds upon God with supreme faith are considered the most perfect in yoga. Unwavering devotion purifies the heart and rapidly burns away ignorance.
Modern Application: Pure passion, dedication, and surrender to a higher purpose can override intellectual shortcomings, often leading to extraordinary mastery and peace in whatever craft you pursue.
